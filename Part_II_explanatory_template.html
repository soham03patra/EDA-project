Part II - Explanatory Analysis of the Ford GoBike Bike-Sharing Dataset¶

by Soham Patra¶

Investigation Overview¶

This presentation explores the February 2019 Ford GoBike trip dataset to understand rider behavior. The analysis focuses on trip duration, user type, age, and gender. The goal is to identify travel patterns and understand how customer characteristics influence bike usage.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset contains information on 183,412 bike-sharing trips made during February 2019 in the San Francisco Bay Area. After data cleaning, missing values and duplicate records were removed, resulting in a high-quality dataset for analysis.

The presentation focuses on the following variables:

  • Trip Duration
  • User Type
  • Rider Age
  • Member Gender

These variables were selected because they provide valuable insights into rider behavior and trip characteristics.

Visualization 1: Distribution of Trip Duration¶

In [8]:
# Plot the distribution of trip duration
plt.figure(figsize=(12,6))

bike_duration = bike[bike['duration_sec'] <= 3600]

bins = np.arange(0,3600+50,50)

plt.hist(bike_duration['duration_sec'], bins=bins, color='steelblue')

plt.title('Distribution of Trip Duration')
plt.xlabel('Trip Duration (Seconds)')
plt.ylabel('Number of Trips')

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

The histogram shows that most bike trips last less than 20 minutes, with the distribution heavily skewed toward shorter durations. Only a small number of trips extend beyond one hour. This indicates that the bike-sharing system is primarily used for short-distance travel and daily commuting.

Customers generally take longer trips and show greater variability, while Subscribers have shorter and more consistent ride durations. This suggests that Subscribers mainly use the service for regular commuting, whereas Customers are more likely to use bikes for occasional or leisure trips.

Conclusion¶

The explanatory analysis highlights several important findings from the Ford GoBike dataset:

  • Most bike trips last less than 20 minutes.
  • Subscribers account for the majority of rides and typically have shorter trip durations.
  • Customers tend to take longer trips than Subscribers.
  • Rider age has only a weak relationship with trip duration.
  • User type is the strongest factor influencing bike trip duration.

These findings suggest that the Ford GoBike system is primarily used for short-distance transportation, particularly by regular subscribers who use the service for commuting.
